# MathML

You can use MathML on School of Haskell.

Here's an example:

```
<math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML" display="block">
  <mfenced open="[" close="]">
    <mrow>
      <mo>&#x2212;<!-- − --></mo>
      <mfrac>
        <msup>
          <mi class="MJX-variant">&#x210F;<!-- ℏ --></mi>
          <mn>2</mn>
        </msup>
        <mrow>
          <mn>2</mn>
          <mi>m</mi>
        </mrow>
      </mfrac>
      <mfrac>
        <msup>
          <mi mathvariant="normal">&#x2202;<!-- ∂ --></mi>
          <mn>2</mn>
        </msup>
        <mrow>
          <mi mathvariant="normal">&#x2202;<!-- ∂ --></mi>
          <msup>
            <mi>x</mi>
            <mn>2</mn>
          </msup>
        </mrow>
      </mfrac>
      <mo>+</mo>
      <mi>V</mi>
    </mrow>
  </mfenced>
  <mi mathvariant="normal">&#x03A8;<!-- Ψ --></mi>
  <mo>=</mo>
  <mi>i</mi>
  <mi class="MJX-variant">&#x210F;<!-- ℏ --></mi>
  <mfrac>
    <mi mathvariant="normal">&#x2202;<!-- ∂ --></mi>
    <mrow>
      <mi mathvariant="normal">&#x2202;<!-- ∂ --></mi>
      <mi>t</mi>
    </mrow>
  </mfrac>
  <mi mathvariant="normal">&#x03A8;<!-- Ψ --></mi>
</math>
```
